## Current Expert Consensus (2024-2026)

### Key Research Findings

**McKinsey (2025):**
- 71% of AI leaders report rapid commoditization
- Competitive advantage shifting from "building AI" to "executing with AI"
- **Key insight:** Tech moats have 6-18 month shelf life; organizational moats still defend 5+ years

**MIT Sloan (2025):**
- Studied 200+ software companies; organizational culture is the primary differentiator
- Companies with strong psychological safety adapt to AI 2x faster
- **Key quote:** "In an AI-mediated market, your people are your only true moat."

**BCG (2024):**
- Classified moats into three tiers: Commoditized (6-18 mo), Defensible (2-3 yrs), Durable (5+ yrs)
- Tech-focused moats: 60% failure rate
- Trust/capability-focused moats: 30% failure rate
- **Key insight:** Moving investment to durable moats sees 3-5x better returns

**Sequoia Capital / a16z (2024-2025):**
- Most "AI startups" are dead; winners are using AI for their core business better
- The moat is distribution + integration + trust, not the AI itself
- **Key quote:** "Companies fighting for AI differentiation are fighting for a shrinking advantage."

## Case Studies: Real-World Moat Erosion (2022-2025)

### ✅ Successfully Adapted

**Stripe**: Tech moat eroded (good APIs everywhere) → Built trust + organizational capability → Now competes on relationships and ecosystem (fintech infrastructure play)

**Adobe**: Generative AI commoditized design tools → Integrated AI into products + leaned into "ethical AI" brand → Now competes on ecosystem and brand, not features

**Apple**: Face constant competition on features/price → Competes on design values + ecosystem + brand → Moats strengthened despite commoditization

### ❌ Failed to Adapt

**Twitter/X**: Network effects eroded because organizational coherence collapsed → Lost trust → Users fled to Bluesky/Threads despite network effects → Shows that network effects are fragile

**Intel**: Scale/process advantage eroded (AMD/TSMC caught up) → Failed to adapt to new era → Lost market share to companies with better organizational agility

**IBM Mainframes**: Proprietary tech + switching costs → Open source Linux commoditized → Had to pivot to services/consulting (organizational capability)

   - *Mitigation:* Framework is robust to moat shifts (kindness/trust/capability are universal). Update framework as new moats emerge.
