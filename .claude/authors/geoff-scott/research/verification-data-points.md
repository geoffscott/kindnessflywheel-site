## How to Verify These Numbers

### Costco & Southwest (Public Companies - Easy to Verify)
1. **SEC EDGAR database:** Search [ticker symbol]'s 10-K annual filings
   - https://www.sec.gov/cgi-bin/browse-edgar
   - Look for "10-K" for most recent fiscal year
   - Revenue in "Consolidated Statements of Earnings"
   - Net income in same statement

2. **Stock prices:** Yahoo Finance or Google Finance
   - Historical price data back to 2016
   - Adjusted for splits and dividends

3. **Earnings calls:** Company investor relations websites
   - Southwest: southwest.com/investor-relations
   - Costco: costco.com/investor-relations

### Stripe (Private - Harder to Verify)
1. **Crunchbase Premium:** Valuation history (requires paid account)
2. **PitchBook:** Valuation and financing history (requires paid account)
3. **Public interviews:** Founders (Patrick and John Collison) discuss revenue/metrics
4. **Investor letters:** Shared with shareholders (not fully public)
5. **Secondary market:** SharesPost, Forge show recent trading prices

### Patagonia (Private - Very Limited Disclosure)
1. **Company announcements:** Patagonia.com/company and press releases
2. **Owner interviews:** Yvon Chouinard has given public interviews disclosing revenue
3. **Industry research:** Market research firms track revenue estimates
4. **Impact reports:** Annual Patagonia environmental and social reports
5. **Note:** Full financial statements are not public; most data is estimated or from owner statements

## Challenge Responses (If Anyone Disputes)

**"Costco isn't really that successful—growth is only 6.8% CAGR"**
- Response: Slow growth isn't failure. Costco's stock returned 550%, market cap grew 5x. Operating at $245B with 2.7% net margin and consistent profitability. Compare to peers: Walmart 4% CAGR (similar), Amazon 28% CAGR (growth different model). Costco is best-in-class for its category with highest member renewal rate.

**"Southwest should be included—it's a successful company"**
- Response: Stock down 40% (objective fact), market cap declined (objective fact), lost money 2020-2023 (SEC filings), 2023 operational crisis destroyed trust (DOT data, NPS drop). By decade-end metrics, Southwest underperformed. It was successful 2016-2019, then failed tests.

**"Stripe is only $2.5B revenue, not comparable to Costco's $245B"**
- Response: Agreed. Stripe is fastest-growing (50%+ CAGR) and highest-growth potential. "Most successful" can mean different things: Costco is success by scale/profitability/consistency; Stripe is success by growth/market dominance in segment. Both true, different metrics.

**"Patagonia isn't "most successful"—it's intentionally limited"**
- Response: Correct. "Most successful" by growth or scale? No. By category leadership, consistency, trust model, and profitability? Yes. Better framing: "Category leader" not "among most successful."

**"Where's your source for Stripe's revenue?"**
- Response: Stripe does not disclose revenue publicly (private company). Estimate of $2.5-3B inferred from: (a) investor letter disclosures from prior rounds, (b) payment volume data, (c) gross margin benchmarks, (d) public founder interviews. Acknowledge this is estimated, not definitive.

**"These NPS scores—where do they come from?"**
- Response: Most NPS data is estimated from brand tracking studies, customer surveys, and public disclosures. Costco's high NPS is well-documented (investor calls, industry reports). Southwest's 2023 drop is documented in DOT complaints and media coverage. Patagonia's high NPS is inferred from brand strength and customer loyalty behavior. Acknowledge uncertainty where it exists.
