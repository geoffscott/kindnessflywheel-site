## The Flip (Core Framing)

The future is a race to the median — businesses all doing what works, little brand loyalty as everyone chases features. Competitors copy, customers follow, then something new appears and the cycle repeats.

**The question that changes everything**: Flip from "how much can I extract per customer?" to "how much can I give this customer?"

- Even "earn" is really "extract" when you're trying to earn at the lowest possible cost to maximize profit
- Giving doesn't have to be expensive. The least expensive thing to give: authentic care and kindness
- But some of it has to be material to be real — not just words

**The mechanism**: Be kind to your employees → they're kind to your customers → customers stay loyal → flywheel spins.

Give employees more than what they need, not less. Still need efficient processes — efficiency and kindness aren't opposed. The shift is in *what you're optimizing for*.

**Key nuance**: Measurement still matters. How busy people are, how well they work together (humans and agents). But measurement in service of flourishing, not extraction.
