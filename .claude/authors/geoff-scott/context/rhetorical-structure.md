# Classical Rhetorical Argument Structure

Reference for Kindness Flywheel posts. Based on Aristotle's *On Rhetoric*.

## The Six Parts

| Part | Latin | Function |
|------|-------|----------|
| **Introduction** | *Exordium* | Hook the audience. Establish tone, urgency (kairos), and credibility (ethos). Make them care. |
| **Background** | *Narratio* | Set the context. What's the situation? What history does the reader need? Create shared reality. |
| **Thesis & Roadmap** | *Propositio & Partitio* | State your claim and briefly preview how you'll prove it. Short — 3-4 sentences. The trailer, not the movie. |
| **Proof** | *Confirmatio* | Your positive case. Evidence, data, examples, stories that support your claim. The body of the argument. |
| **Refutation** | *Refutatio* | Address counterarguments and objections. Acknowledge them honestly, then show why your position holds. |
| **Conclusion** | *Peroratio* | Summarize, make the emotional appeal, call to action. Leave the audience moved, not just informed. |

## The Four Appeals (woven throughout)

- **Ethos** — Credibility. Why should they trust you? **Use lightly.** Credibility comes from the quality of the thinking, not from listing qualifications. Over-emphasizing credentials loses credibility with our audience. Show competence through the work, don't assert it.
- **Logos** — Logic. Data, evidence, reasoning.
- **Pathos** — Emotion. Stories, lived experience, what's at stake.
- **Kairos** — Timing. Why now? Why is this urgent?

The strongest arguments weave all four appeals together rather than relying on one.

## Contemporary Models

### Toulmin (1958) — Evidence Architecture

Stephen Toulmin developed this model out of frustration with classical logic. It maps how arguments actually work in practice, not in theory.

| Component | Function |
|-----------|----------|
| **Claim** | The position you're asserting |
| **Data** | The evidence supporting the claim |
| **Warrant** | The unstated assumption connecting data to claim — the "why does this evidence prove this point?" |
| **Backing** | Additional support if the warrant isn't self-evident |
| **Qualifier** | Limits on the claim: "sometimes," "in most cases," "for mid-market companies" |
| **Rebuttal** | Conditions under which the claim doesn't hold |

**Value for KF:** Forces you to expose your warrants (unstated assumptions) and qualify your claims honestly. When we say "trust drives performance," the warrant is "employees who feel trusted work harder and stay longer." That warrant needs its own evidence. Toulmin discipline prevents overclaiming.

### Rogerian (1970) — Collaborative Argument

Carl Rogers was a psychologist, not a rhetorician. His approach was adapted for writing by Young, Becker, and Pike. The core idea: when your audience has strong opposing views, confrontation makes them stop listening. Build a bridge instead.

| Step | Function |
|------|----------|
| **Present the problem** | Neutrally. No thesis yet. |
| **Validate the opposing view** | Show you genuinely understand it. Concede where it's valid. |
| **Present your view** | As valid under certain conditions — not as the only truth |
| **Find compromise or synthesis** | A position both sides can live with |

**Key principle:** "You must run the risk of being changed yourself." If you're not genuinely open to being wrong, the audience can tell.

**Value for KF:** Our target audience includes skeptical executives who have built careers on extractive models. Telling them they're wrong won't work. Showing we understand why extraction is rational — and then presenting an alternative that serves their interests better — is Rogerian in spirit.

**Has no fixed structure.** Rogers deliberately avoided conventional persuasive structures because "these devices tend to produce a sense of threat." The structure emerges from the writer, topic, and audience.

## The Kindness Flywheel House Style

What we've naturally arrived at is a hybrid:

- **Classical structure** — clear sections, conviction, a thesis worth defending
- **Rogerian spirit** — genuine openness, conceding counterarguments have merit, inviting collaboration rather than demanding agreement
- **Toulmin discipline** — qualifying claims, exposing warrants, being honest about what the evidence actually supports vs. what we believe

In practice, this means:

1. **State what you believe with conviction** (Classical propositio)
2. **Show the evidence honestly, including its limits** (Toulmin qualifiers and warrants)
3. **Name the strongest counterarguments and leave them standing** (Rogerian concession)
4. **Frame open questions as invitations, not weaknesses** (Rogerian collaboration)
5. **Commit to being changed by what we find** (Rogerian core principle)

This is not a formula. It's a posture: confident enough to assert, honest enough to qualify, open enough to be wrong.

## Application to Kindness Flywheel Posts

Most posts should follow this structure, adapted to length and tone:

- **Short posts** may compress narratio and propositio into a single section
- **Story-driven posts** (#Practice) may lead with narratio and let the thesis emerge from the story
- **Technical posts** (#Technology) may have a heavier confirmatio with code/architecture examples
- **The refutatio matters** — acknowledging counterarguments honestly is core to the publication's credibility. Don't skip it.
